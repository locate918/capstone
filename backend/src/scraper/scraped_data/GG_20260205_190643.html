Iron & Wine will headline Guthrie Green’s 2026 Off the Cuff Concert Series with a FREE show on Sunday, October 25. The performance is part of the band’s world tour in support of their upcoming album, Hen’s Tooth, set for release on February 27. Known for their intimate songwriting and warm, genre-blending sound, Iron & Wine seamlessly weave indie folk with elements of folk rock, Americana, and lo-fi.

ABOUT IRON & WINE

\n

WEBSITE | INSTAGRAM | SPOTIFY | APPLE MUSIC | YOUTUBE

\n

Sam Beam is a singer-songwriter who has been creating music as Iron & Wine for over a decade. Through the course of eight albums, numerous EPs and singles, and the initial volumes of an Archive Series, Iron & Wine has captured the emotion and imagination of listeners with distinctly cinematic songs.

\n

 

\n

Hen’s Teeth is Sam Beam’s eighth full-length album and a rare-feeling gift, “an impossible thing, but it’s real.” Recorded alongside 2024’s Light Verse with the same band in Laurel Canyon, the two records are sibling works: where Light Verse is airy and impressionistic, Hen’s Teeth is earthier, darker, and more tactile.

\n

Built quickly and spontaneously, the album leans into folk rock, jazz-inflected folk, and Tropicalia, with songs that revel in physical closeness, devotion, and emotional intensity. Featuring contributions from I’m With Her and harmonies from Beam’s daughter Arden, Hen’s Teeth captures an artist at his most free, collaborative, curious, and deeply in love with the act of making music.

\n

 

\n

 

\n

ABOUT DUG
DUG is a folk duo made up of Conor “Lorkin” O’Reilly and Jonny Pickett, whose chemistry feels decades deep despite forming in 2023. Drawing from musical traditions on both sides of the Atlantic, their sound blends the spirit of American folk troubadours with the storytelling and melodic sensibility of Irish folk.

\n

Born across America and Scotland and now based in Ireland, the duo’s lived musical history shows in their deft, banjo-and-resonator-driven arrangements and sharp, warm songwriting. Their songs balance humor with heart, pairing foot-tapping grooves with moments of longing and reflection—inviting, playful, and deeply human.

\n

 

\n

Signed to Claddagh Records in 2024, DUG are an unapologetically modern folk act with a growing live following. With their debut album, the duo spent much of 2025 touring internationally, including festival dates across Ireland, the U.S., and Australia.

\n



ABOUT KEN POMEROY

\n

Ken Pomeroy writes songs that cut straight to the heart—sometimes with just a single line. A Cherokee artist raised in Moore, Oklahoma, her music blends empathetic storytelling with rootsy, indie-folk textures and a soprano that can soothe or devastate in equal measure. Guided by ancestral wisdom and lived experience, Pomeroy creates deeply cathartic songs that offer both hurt and hope.

\n

Her latest album, Cruel Joke (Rounder Records, 2025), is a brutally honest, proudly Native American collection that has quickly drawn attention. Her music has appeared on the Twisters soundtrack and Hulu’s Reservation Dogs, and she’s toured with artists like Lukas Nelson, Iron & Wine, and American Aquarium, with performances at Newport Folk and Telluride Bluegrass Festivals.
